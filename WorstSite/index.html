Natto
Natto is a traditional Japanese dish made from fermented soybeans. It has a distinctive taste and aroma, often described as strong, pungent,and somewhat earthy. What makes natto particularly divisive is its slimy texture, which results from the fermentation process. The beans are coated in a sticky, viscous substance called mucilage, giving natto a unique and somewhat challenging mouthfeel. Additionally, natto has a strong odor that some find off-putting, reminiscent of cheese or ammonia.

Surströmming
Surströmming is a Swedish delicacy consisting of fermented Baltic sea herring. What sets surströmming apart—and often earns it the label of being disgusting—is its incredibly strong and pungent odor. The fermentation process creates a cocktail of volatile compounds, including butyric acid and hydrogen sulfide, giving surströmming an overpowering smell often likened to rotten eggs, ammonia, or even feces. The taste is also intense and acquired, characterized by a combination of sourness, saltiness, and umami flavors. The texture of the fish is soft and mushy due to the fermentation, further contributing to the divisive nature of this delicacy.

English Trifle

Christmas Tinner
Christmas Tinner is a novelty product marketed as a complete Christmas dinner in a tin, designed for those who want to enjoy the holiday meal without the hassle of cooking and cleaning up. It contains layers of various traditional Christmas foods, including turkey, potatoes, gravy, cranberry sauce, stuffing, and even dessert like Christmas pudding, all compacted into a single can.
